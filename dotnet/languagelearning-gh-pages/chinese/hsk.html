What Is HSK?

HSK (汉语水平考试 / Hànyǔ shuǐpíng kǎoshì) is the People's Republic of China's standardised Chinese proficiency test. For general information about the test, see Hanyu Shuiping Kaoshi (on Wikipedia) and Hanban's website.
HSK was revised in 2010. See the Comparison of New and Old HSK Test for a basic overview of similarity and differences.

Some test centres offer computer-based tests for HSK levels 4, 5 and 6. If you decide to take a compter-based HSK test, make sure you find out in advance what input method will be used on the computer (so you can familiarise yourself with it in advnace) and, if you are not used to read Chinese texts in a variety of font faces, which font face will be used to display text. See the question What's the format of official “online” HSK tests? on Chinese Language Stack Exchange.

HSK and CEFR

Hanban's correspondence table for HSK levels and CEFR levels is misleading. (See the table on Hanban's HSK page.) The following table compares Hanban's claimed levels and those defined by the Association of Teachers of Chinese in German Speaking Countries (Fachverband Chinesisch or FaCh).

HSK Level Required vocabulary CEFR (Hanban) Actual CEFR (FaCh)
HSK 1 150 words (pinyin) A1 -
HSK 2 300 words (pinyin) A2 A1.1
HSK 3 600 words B1 A1
HSK 4 1200 words B2 A2
HSK 5 2500 words C1 B1
HSK 6 over 5000 words C2 B2

Grammar for HSK

Most grammar books for learners of Chinese provide no information on the HSK level they are relevant to. Grammar books that focus on the grammar required for the HSK tests are still quite rare.

  • How to study Chinese grammar for the HSK exams (03.12.2013): a blog post with some tips and links to resources.
  • Common Chinese Patterns 330 by HSK Level (12.06.2013): this blog post classifies the grammar points from the book Common Chinese Patterns 330 (Sinolingua 2009, ISBN9787802006478) by HSK level. The classification is based on the characters used in the patterns, not on the grammar that is required by the HSK tests. Since this book was not written with HSK in mind, the example sentences ignore the HSK levels for vocabulary. The book is in Chinese and English, without pinyin transcriptions.
  • AllSet Learning:
    • A1 grammar points (claimed to be roughly equivalent to HSK Level 1);
    • A2 grammar points (claimed to be roughly equivalent to HSK Level 2);
    • B1 grammar points (claimed to be roughly equivalent to HSK Level 3);
    • B2 grammar points (claimed to be roughly equivalent to HSK Level 4);
    • C1 grammar points (incomplete; claimed to be roughly equivalent to HSK Level 5).

General HSK Preparation Materials

  • Hanban: 资源中心--汉语考试服务网: page where you can download sample exam papers. This is useful because the real exams contain many words that you won't find in many of the books and websites that help you prepare for HSK.
  • Shasha, a Chinese teacher from Hanbridge Mandarin, has three YouTube playlists:
    • HSK Level 3 Preparation.
    • HSK Level 4 Practice (a few videos to practice listening skills).
    • HSK Level 5 Practice (videos that cover reading, writing and listening).
  • Peking University: Chinese for HSK 1, Chinese for HSK 2, Chinese for HSK 3 Part 1 and Chinese for HSK 3 Part 2 are four online courses hosted on Coursera. The courses make up a so-called specialization called Chinese For HSK (Level 1–3). The first course requires 4 to 6 hours of study per week for six weeks; information about the time commitment for the other courses is not available. The courses are not for free but have a seven-day trial period.
  • Beijing Language and Culture University Press (BLCUP): HSK Standard Course: a series of books for the six levels of HSK that are claimed to be suitable both for language schools and for self-taught learners.
  • HSK Academy: website with learning materials by the French organisation HSK Academy.
  • HSK 东西: website with resources to prepare for the HSK tests.
  • Li, Yinhua; Buchta, Katrin: HSK-Übungsbuch (Stufen 1 und 2). Hamburg: Buske, 2011. ISBN 978-3-87548-594-3. (240 pages)
    The first part of this book covers HSK 1, the second part covers HSK 2. The first two chapters of each part contain practice tests for listening skills and reading skills, respectively. In addition to solutions, the book also provides tips and transcriptions. The first two chapters are followed by example exams, followed by solutions to these exams. The first part ends with a word list for HSK 1. The second part ends with a word list for HSK 2, followed by a short section grammar. The book also contains a CD with audio materials for the listening skills section (including the example exams).
  • Li, Yinhua; Buchta, Katrin: HSK-Übungsbuch (Stufe 3). Hamburg: Buske, 2013. ISBN 978-3-87548-595-0. (194 pages)
    This book contains tips and example questions for the HSK 3 test. It also contains two example exams: one at the start of the book, and another one in the middle (after the sections that cover the three parts of the exam individually). In addition, it has a section of almost 30 pages on grammar, which contains grammatical explanations, grammar exercises and solutions to these exercises. The last section of the book contains a list of measure words and a alphabetically organised vocabulary list (600 words) for HSK 3. There is a CD with audio materials for section 2 (listening skills) and for the two model tests. The two model tests are slightly more difficult than the real exam and help you determine whether you are ready for the test.
  • Huang, Hefei; Ziethen, Dieter: Vorbereitung HSK-Prüfung. HSK 3. Second edition. Gröbenzell: Hefei Huang Verlag, 2012. ISBN 978-3-940497-41-3. (304 pages)
    This book is part of a series of HSK preparation books. The first three short chapters provide a general introduction to HSK, guidance on how to use the book, a vocabulary list (600 words) and a 12-page grammar review. The following chapters provide practice materials for each part of the test (4 chapters for listening comprehension, 2 chapters for reading comprehension, 2 chapters for writing). The book has four appendices: Appendix A provides a 47-page long grammar overview for HSK 3 (without exercises), Appendix B gives a list of measure words, Appendix C lists characters with more than one pronunciation (and meaning), and Appendix D gives a short list of similar and therefore potentially confusable words. The book comes with an MP3 CD with audio materials for the chapters on listening comprehension.
  • Huang, Hefei; Ziethen, Dieter: Ziel HSK4 – Chinesische Lesetexte mit Vokabeln und Grammatik – Teil 1. Gröbenzell: Hefei Huang Verlag, 2014. ISBN 978-3-940497-54-3. (286 pages)
    Ziel HSK4 – Schriftliche Übungen für Grammatik und Wortschatz – Teil 1. Gröbenzell: Hefei Huang Verlag, 2014. ISBN 978-3-940497-56-7. (324 pages)
    These two books constitute the first part of a set of books that prepare learners for the text-oriented parts of HSK 4. Each book contains thirty lessons; the exercises in the second book are intended for use with the corresponding lessons in the first book. The exercise book also has two audio CDs.
  • Huang, Hefei; Ziethen, Dieter: Ziel HSK4 – Chinesische Lesetexte mit Vokabeln und Grammatik – Teil 2. Gröbenzell: Hefei Huang Verlag, 2015. ISBN 978-3-940497-62-8. (288 pages)
    Ziel HSK4 – Schriftliche Übungen für Grammatik und Wortschatz – Teil 2. Gröbenzell: Hefei Huang Verlag, 2016. ISBN 978-3-940497-63-5. (304 pages)
    These two books contain another set of 30 lessons. The exercise book has two audio CDs.
  • 刘云 (主编/editor): 新汉语水平考试 HSK (四级)攻略：听力. Peking University Press, 2011. (259 pages + CD-ROM) ISBN 978-7-301-17848-5.
    A book to practice listening skills for HSK 4, entirely in Chinese that requires (at least) a reading level at HSK 4. In other words, you need to be familiar with HSK 4 vocabulary before you start studying the book.

General Tips for HSK

  • HSK 3 to HSK 4 in 3 Months (15 June 2015) and “HSK 3 to HSK 4 in 3 Months” - Ollie’s challenge follow-up!!! (20 November 2015): two blog posts by a language learner who set himself the challenge to go from HSK 3 to HSK 4 in 3 months instead of 1 year, and what he did to achieve this. (It actually took a bit longer than 3 months, though clearly less than 6 months.)
  • Scott Young (and Olle Linge): How to reach a decent level of Chinese in 100 days (June 2014).
    See also Learning Chinese over 3½ Months (YouTube): this is a 12-minute documentary about Scott Young and Vat Jaiswal's experiment in language learning. See also the blog posts The Year Without English and Looking Back at a Year (Almost) Without English (01.09.2014).
    While these articles and the video are not about HSK, they also focus on rapid improvement.
  • FluentU has several blog posts about the HSK exams, including the following: 24 Battle Tested Tips for Dominating the HSK (October 2014), 54 Fantastic Resources to Help You Prepare for the HSK (October 2014), The Insider Guide to Acing the HSK Exam (October 2014).
  • Qin Chen: How to Prepare HSK Exam, article on DigMandarin (05.07.2013, updated 18.02.2015). This short article provides some tips and lists several books that can help you prepare for HSK: HSK Essentials, Chinese Learning Cards for HSK, HSK Growing Training, Say it in Chinese, HSK Listening Key Words, Express Way to HSK, HSK dictionary, An Intensive Course of HSK.
  • Hanbridge Mandarin: HSK learning tips (collection of articles).
  • Top 10 des meilleurs conseils pour l’examen HSK, LTL Mandarin School, 20.04.2015.
  • Le HSK: L'examen de chinois, They call me stranger, 09.07.2017.
  • How long between each HSK level for learning Mandarin? (a question on Language Learning Stack Exchange).
  • Hu, Elliot: How long does it take to pass each level of the HSK at constant effort? (a question on Quora). Hu assumes the following figures:
    • HSK 1: 100–150 hours of study time.
    • HSK 2: 300 hours of study time.
    • HSK 3: 600 hours of study time.
    • HSK 4: 1200 hours of study time.
    • HSK 5: 2500 hours of study time.
    • HSK 6: 5000 hours of study time.
    Hu wrongly assumes that Hanban's mapping between HSK levels and CEFR levels is correct. As mentioned above, this is misleading.
