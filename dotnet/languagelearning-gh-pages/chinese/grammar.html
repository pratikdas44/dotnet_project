Grammar Books

  • Herzberg, Qin Xue; Herzberg, Larry: Basic Patterns of Chinese Grammar. Berkeley, California: Stone Bridge Press, 2011. (127 pages) ISBN 978-1-933330-89-1.
    The authors have taught Chinese for several decades; Qin Xue is a native speaker of Chinese. The book covers basic aspects of Chinese grammar, such as word order, time expressions, verbs, the particle 了, the particle 的, conjunctions, subordinate clauses, how to express “can”, etcetera. Each section of the book has example sentences in English with translations in Chinese characters and pinyin. It discusses many errors that native speakers of English make when translating literally from English to Chinese. The book does not contain exercises.
  • Po-Ching, Yip; Rimmington, Don; Zhang, Xiaoming; Henson, Rachel; Yip, Li Quzhen: Basic Chinese: A Grammar and Workbook. Second edition. London: Routledge, 2010. (376 pages) ISBN 9780415472159.
  • Po-Ching, Yip; Rimmington, Don: Intermediate Chinese: A Grammar and Workbook. Second edition. London: Routledge, 2009. (384 pages) ISBN 978-0-415-48631-6.
    This book consists of 25 units. Each unit consists of a section with grammar explanations and a section with exercises. An answer key is available at the back of the book. There are units about 把 structures, 被 structures, the particles 的, 得 and 地, the sentence particle 了, and many other aspects of Chinese grammar. In addition to an answer key, the bookk also contains a 58-page vocabulary list and a glossary of grammatical terms and an index. The answer key does not always provide every possible correct answer, so it can be worthwhile to check your answers with a native speaker of Chinese.
  • Huang, Chu-Ren; Shi, Dingxu (editors): A Reference Grammar of Chinese. Cambridge University Press, 2016. (611 pages) ISBN 9780521181051.
  • Xu Dan (徐丹): Initiation à la syntaxe chinoise. Des repères et des analyses pour écrire et parler juste. Deuxième édition, ouvrage revu et actualisé. Collection Langues-Inalco. Paris: L'Asiathèque – maison des langues du monde, 2010. ISBN 978-2-36057-005-8.
  • Huang, James C.-T.; Li, Y.-H. Audrey; Li, Yafei: The Syntax of Chinese. Cambridge Syntax Guides. Cambridge University Press, 2009. (404 pages) ISBN 9780521599580.

Usage

The following books are situated on the borderline between grammar and vocabulary:

  • 陈如 (Chén Rú); 朱晓亚 (Zhū Xiǎoyà): Common Chinese Patterns 330: 汉语常用格式330例. Beijing: Sinolingua, 2010. (330 pages) ISBN 978-7-80200-647-8.
    This book presents one “pattern” per page. For each pattern, the book provides an explanation in Chinese and English, example sentences with an English translation, two short dialogues that use the pattern, and one or two exercises. The book does not use pinyin. Some examples of patterns: A比B~多了; A比B更/还~; 不~也~; 都~了; 算了; 我说~; 要不是~; and 重~轻~.
  • Poizat-Xie, Honghua (谢红华); Zufferey, Nicolas: Mots chinois, mots français. Manuel d'analyse lexicale pour francophones 1. Collection Langues-Inalco. Paris: L'Asiathèque – maison des langues du monde, 2010. ISBN 978-2-36057-002-7.
    This book focuses on difference in usage of semantically related words and expressions, for example, the difference between 按照 and 根据, the difference between 帮, 帮助 and 帮忙, the difference between 别的, 另 and 其他, etcetera.
  • Poizat-Xie, Honghua (谢红华); Wyss, Marie: Nuances et subtilités de la langue chinoise. Manuel d'analyse lexicale pour francophones 2. Collection Langues-Inalco. Paris: L'Asiathèque – maison des langues du monde, 2010. ISBN 978-2-36057-003-4.
    This book focuses on difference in usage of related words and expressions, for example, the difference between 爱 and 喜欢, the difference between 但是, 可是, 却 and related conjunctions, the difference between 东西 and 事, etcetera.
