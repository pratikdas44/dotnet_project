Getting Started

Tips before you begin learning Korean.

  • Why YOU Should Learn Korean | 4 Reasons to Learn Korean: video on the YouTube channel “Learn Korean with GO! Billy Korean”. This video points out that the US government recognizes Korean as one of the most difficult languages to learn for native speakers of English, requiring at least 2,200 hours of study to become proficient. (2,200 hours corresponds roughly studying 8 hours per day for 9 monhts.) The reasons give are the following: finding a job that requires Korean, making friends, dating a Korean, and enjoying k-drama and k-pop.
  • How can I use the weeks before starting a beginner’s Korean course?: question on Korean Language Stack Exchange.
  • Basic steps to take when learning Korean: question on Korean Language Stack Exchange.
  • Shanna: How to self-study Korean for Beginners, Hangukdrama & Korean, 22.06.2011.
  • Comment apprendre le coréen soi-même (YouTube, 10 minutes, 24.10.2014). This French video discusses resources that beginners can use: the books by Ganada (Korean for Foreigners, textbook and workbook), the books by Sogang University, Korean Picture Dictionary (for basic vocabulary), J'aime le coréen! by Soyoung Yun-Roger, and 50 règles essentielles coréen by Stéphane Couralet. She also used a beginners' French language course for native speakers of Korean.
  • Learn Korean Online: How to Choose the Best Course, 90 Day Korean (no date).
  • Korni: Koreanisch Starter-Kit, Koreanisch-lernen.net, 13.02.2017.

Learning Korean

  • Sogang University: Sogang Korean Program: this is an online course that covers introdcutory Korean and intermediate Korean. The content was developed in the late 1990s and early 200s, heavily relies on Adobe Flash and looks a bit dated from a visual point of view. The first lesson assumes that you can read Hangul.
  • Learn Korean with GO! Billy Korean is a YouTube that started in 2012. The channels has playlists such as Learn Korean – Full Lessons with PDFs (over 90 videos), Tips and Tricks for Learning Korean, Learn Hangul – Start here! and Korean Phrases and Idioms (over 50 videos). The channel's creator also wrote the book Korean Made Simple.
  • Rocket Languages: Rocket Korean: an online course. See also the review on AudibleLanguages.com.
  • TalkToMeInKorean (TTMIK) & TalkToMeInKorean (TTMIK) on YouTube: an online course (more than 1,000 lessons) and learning materials (the site sells textbooks and workbooks for different levels, and books to help you prepare for TOPIk). Much of the content on the site is available under a Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivs 3.0 Unported License.
    The YouTube channel has playlists such as Korean Pronunciation Guide (40 videos), Listening in Slow Korean (6 videos), Simple Korean Word Lessons and Advanced Korean Lessons.
    See also the materials on the Korean Wiki Project.
  • Learn Korean with KoreanClass101.com: an online course that also has a YouTube channel.
  • Korean Unnie 한국언니 is a YouTube channel by a native speaker of Korean. It contains playlists such as Learn Korean with Korean Unnie 한국언니와 한국어 배우기 (more than 80 videos) and Learn Korean with Korean Songs 노래로 한국어 배우기
  • Minji Teaches Korean is a YouTube channel with playlists such as Basic Korean Lessons with Minji, Learn Korean Phrases with Minji, Learn Korean Words with Minji and Explore Korean Culture and Food.
  • KoreaFever is a YouTube channel by Han-Na ((한나) with playlists such as Korean Alphabet & Number System, Learn Korean | TalkTalk Korean (more than 20 videos) and Phrases.
  • Introduction to Korean: a free online course by Hanyang University (Seoul) hosted on FutureLearn. The course is six weeks long and requires 3 hours of study per week. Course description:

    This course will introduce you to the Korean language, and present and explain Korean culture, enabling you to have real Korean conversations.

    During the course, you will learn Korean phrases, greetings, vocabulary, numbers, spelling and grammar. It is composed not only of the explanation and presentation of culture but also the activities related to Korean language.

    By the end of the course, you will be able to have a real Korean conversation with your newly acquired knowledge of the Korean language. This course will give you a taste of language skills and Korean cultures.

    The first session started on 29 May 2017. The second session starts on 25 September 2017.
  • Pathway to Spoken Korean is a website by the National East Asian Languages Resource Center at Ohio State University.
  • Yeolshimi.fr is a French website with resources for learning Korean.
  • How to Study Korean: in spite of its title, this site is not just about how to study Korean; it also provides lessons and learning materials that you can use to supplement your own learning. (The site does not claim that it offers everything to make you fluent in Korean. See also the comments on Korean StackExchange: Does HowToStudyKorean.com really do what it says?.)
  • Motivate Korean: a YouTube channel maintained by Jeremy Brinkerhoff, who also created two Korean Language courses on Udemy: Learn Korean! Start Speaking Korean Now! and Perfect Your Korean Pronunciation - Actually Sound Korean.
  • Korean from Zero is a website with three courses, each with a workbook. The PDF versions of the books are free of charge.
  • Real Korean is a YouTube playlist that contains short Korean language lessons. The playlist is part of the channel Allyfunshow, which contains a lot of content in Korean (including English language lessons for Koreans).
  • Cours de coréen: short playlist of YouTube videos that introduce you to the basics of Korean (in French).
  • Learn Korean in India: a website created by a Korean language learner and educator who got a Bachelors degree in Korean language from JNU, Delhi and a Masters degree in Korean Language Education from Seoul National University, South Korea.
  • BBC Languages: A Guide to Korean (archived site).
  • Tests # livres sur la Corée: playlist with over 20 videos that review books on Korea and the Korean language (in French).
  • koreanstudentblog: blog of a native speaker of Danish who is learning Korean as his/her seventh language.
  • Hangukdrama & Korean: website by Shanna, a Singaporean who has been learning Korean since 2008 (and Japanese since 2012).

Korean Language Proficiency Tests

There are two important official Korean language proficiency tests: TOPIK and KLTP. The following resources are about both tests:

  • Korea.net: Korean Language Tests.
  • LearnKoreanWords.com: Korean Language Test.
  • KLPT vs. TOPIK, Hangukdrama & Korean, 02.05.2010.

Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK)

Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK) is an official Korean proficiency test for non-native speakers of Korean.

  • Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK): article on Wikipedia.
  • Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK): the official website.
  • Ostasiatisches Seminar, Freie Universität Berlin: Information zur TOPIK-Prüfung, 21.01.2013.
  • Koreanistik, Universität Tübingen: Test of Proficiency in Korean [TOPIK] in Tübingen.
  • TOPIK Guide: website that sells e-books and videos to help learners prepare for TOPIK. It also provides past test papers, a study guide and mock tests. (The company was founded by Satish Chandra Satyarthi from India, who has taught Hindi to Koreans, and Korean to Indians .
    See also TOPIK Guide on YouTube.
  • MasterTOPIK is a YouTube channel associated with the website MasterTOPIK.com, which sells online TOPIK preparation courses. The YouTube channel gives you an idea of the video content in the courses. These videos typically have no subtitles. See for example 한국어 첫 단계 – 문법 (The First Step for Korean Language – Grammar). For an example video that uses English, see 한국어 초급 I 정규 과정 1강-영어 English(Korean for beginners I)
  • Aaron: 120 Days to TOPIK #4 – Learn 3600 Words in 4 Months Practicing only 30 Minutes Per Day!, Key to Korean, 25.06.2015.
  • Is the TOPIK 1-6 equivalent for CEFR A1 to C2? This question was posted on Korean Language Stack Exchange in November 2018.

Korean Language Proficiency Test (KLPT)

The Korean Language Proficiency Test or KLPT (세계한국말인증시험) is another proficience test for non-native speakers of Korean. KLPT appears to be aimed at people who are looking for a job in Korea.

  • Korean Language Proficiency Test (KLPT): article on Wikipedia.
  • Korea Educational Testing Service (KETS) / 한국어능력평가시험: the official website, in Korean.
