Dutch is essentially a mixture of German and English spoken in a Swedish accent. Estel Telcontar (in John Cowan's Essentialist Explanations)

Dutch is a West Germanic language, a subdivision of the Germanic languages that also includes English, German, Afrikaans, Frisian and Yiddish. (Danish, Swedish and Norwegian are also Germanic languages, but belong to a different branch, the North Germanic languages.)

Dutch is an official language in the Netherlands and Belgium. The Dutch-speaking part of Belgium is known as Flanders, so some people refer to the language spoken there as Flemish. However, in spite of some differences in pronunciation and vocabulary, the Flemish and the Dutch speak the same language. They use the same spelling rules, dictionaries and grammars, and they have set up a joint organisation called the Nederlandse Taalunie (Dutch Language Union). The term “Flemish” is best used to refer to the dialects spoken in Flanders or to language features that only occur in Flanders but not in the Netherlands.

Learning Dutch Online

  • FutureLearn: Introduction to Dutch: a free online course that teaches the basics of Dutch in three weeks. The course content was created by the University of Groningen in the Netherlands. The current “session” started in April 2018; the next session starts on 13 August 2018. (The previous sessions started on 14 November 2016, 27 February 2017 and 26 February 2018.)
    (The course uses Quizlet for vocabulary learning, so will also need to register on that site.)
  • FutureLearn: Introduction to Frisian: a free online course that teaches the basics of Dutch in three weeks. The course content was created by the University of Groningen in the Netherlands. The next “session” starts on 5 March 2018. (The previous sessions started on 26 September 2016 and 27 March 2017.)
    Frisian is closely related to Dutch but is considered as a language in its own right.
  • Niederländisch lernen im Internet: eine Übersicht von hilfreichen Sites: overview of online resources for people who speak German.
  • Niederländisch lernen online: a course with 100 lessons (30 of which are free of charge) with MP3 files spoken by native speakers of Dutch. The course is accompanied by a book.
  • Babbel.com: Online Niederländisch lernen.
  • Niederländisch Nachhilfe Online: commercial tutoring and support for native speakers of German who are learning Dutch, especially at secondary school.
  • Cours de néerlandais gratuits: free online course by Laurent Camus, a French teacher of English.

Dutch (Netherlands)

  • Learn Dutch with DutchPod101.com (on YouTube) and its associated website provide resources to learn Dutch (as spoken in the Netherlands).
  • Learn Dutch (on YouTube) and its associated website by Bart De Pau also provide resources to learn Dutch (as spoken in the Netherlands).

Dutch (Belgium)

  • TAALBLAD.BE is an electronic magazine in Dutch for people who are learning Dutch as a foreign language. The website's content is available under a Creative Commons licence.
  • Oefen hier je Nederlands: a website where non-native speakers can practice their Dutch language skills.
  • NEDBOX: Oefen je Nederlands online: a website where non-native speakers can practice their Dutch language skills. It is not a Dutch language course. (The website was developed by the KULeuven and several other partners.)
  • Huis van het Nederlands (Brussel): De Taalgarage - adviespunt voor zelfstudie: contact point for questions relating to learning Dutch through self-learning. The page Taalgarage - selectie materialen lists several types of resources to learn Dutch on your own: course books, grammars, vocabulary books, pronunciation guides, spelling guides, and books about the usage of Dutch in a professional context.
  • Learn Dutch... in Dutch 1: start to read, speak and write: an online course by Alain de Raymond that is hosted on Udemy.
  • Learn Dutch with Alain: YouTube channel by Alain de Raymond that was created in February 2019. See also the associated site Learn Dutch - Learn Any Languages.

Grammar

  • Vlekkeloos Nederlands, Taalbeheersing A2. Taalniveau 1F en 2F. Uitgeverij Pak. ISBN 9789077018569. Answer key (Antwoordenboek): ISBN 9789077018569-A.
    This set of books covers the spelling of verbs, spelling in general, punctuation, style, parts of speech and phrases. Each chapter contains exercises.
  • Vlekkeloos Nederlands, Taalbeheersing B1 (Taalniveau 2F). Uitgeverij Pak. (256 pages) ISBN 9789077018590. Answer key (Antwoordenboek): ISBN 9789077018590-A.
    In addition to grammar rules, this book also covers spelling, style and punctuation. Each chapter contains exercises.
  • Vlekkeloos Nederlands, Taalbeheersing B2 (Taalniveau 3F en 4F). Uitgeverij Pak. (244 pages) ISBN 9789077018309. Answer key (Antwoordenboek): ISBN 9789077018309-A.
    In addition to grammar rules, this book also covers spelling, style and punctuation. Each chapter contains exercises.
  • Vlekkeloos Nederlands voor iedereen. Uitgeverij Pak. ISBN 9789077018750. This is a reference work that can be used for self study. Each chapter ends with a set of exercises; the book also contains an answer key. The book should help you reach level C1 or C2.
  • Fontein, A. M.; Pescher-ter-Meer, A.: Nederlandse Grammatica voor Anderstaligen. NCB Uitgeverij. (287 pages) ISBN ISBN: 9789055170142. This book contains chapters on topics such as spelling, the noun, the adjective, the article, the verb, the demonstrative pronoun, the adverb and syntax.
  • Nl.ver-taal.com: Grammatica-oefeningen: this page contains a few grammar exercises for various levels.
  • Ik schrijf beter (Ingrid Mouthaan): Oefenen | grammatica: a larger set of grammar exercises.
  • Taaltelefoon.be: Grammatica: a list of basic grammar tips provided by a service of the Flemish government.
  • De Elektronische ANS: digital version of the Algemene Nederlandse Spraakkunst (ANS), the most authoritative reference on the grammar of Dutch.
  • Zwart, Jan-Wouter: The Syntax of Dutch. Cambridge Syntax Guides. Cambridge University Press, 2014. (418 pages) ISBN 9781107682337.
    Note that this book is aimed at students of linguistics, not at regular language learners.

    This book belongs to a category of publications devoted to taalzuivering (literally “language purification”). It is a reference work for matters of grammar and vocabulary, which are discussed in short entries that are organised alphabetically. It is aimed at native speakers of Dutch, especially those whose usage of standard Dutch has been influenced by dialects and foreign languages (especially French and English).
  • Permentier, Ludo: Stijlboek. Voor wie helder wil schrijven. Derde, gecatualiseerde editie. Roeselare: Roularta Books, 2008. ISBN 978 90 8679 124 8.
    This book originates from the rules and conventions used by journalists working for the Flemish newspapers De Standaard and Het Nieuwsblad. It contains sections on journalistic genres, writing style, common grammatical problems, spelling and vocabulary. It is also used as a reference work and guide by non-journalists.
